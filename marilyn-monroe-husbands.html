Marilyn Monroe husbands: All about her husbands

Read the following article and you’ll get to know Marilyn Monroe husbands

Marilyn Monroe who was celebrated as a larger-than-life sex symbol, sought real love just like anyone else.

Marilyn Monroe husbands

She married three times during her far-too-short life: first to hometown love James Dougherty, then to baseball star Joe DiMaggio, then to playwright Arthur Miller.

Married James Dougherty in 1942.

In 1942, she wed James Dougherty. Marilyn, at the tender age of 16, entered into matrimony with her first spouse, who was 21 years old at the time.

However, their marriage lasted only until 1946.

Interestingly, Dougherty, who happened to be her neighbor, also served as a police officer with the LAPD, according to information from Today.

Their relationship faced challenges when he joined the Merchant Marines, and she embarked on her career in modeling, as reported by People.

Notably, Dougherty authored a book in 1997 titled “To Norma Jeane with Love,” sharing his perspective on life with Marilyn.

 Married Joe DiMaggio in 1954.

Marilyn’s second spouse, the former Yankees baseball icon Joe DiMaggio, entered her life after she had achieved significant success with two of her most famous projects, “Gentlemen Prefer Blondes” and “How To Marry a Millionaire,” according to Today.

Their paths crossed when Marilyn was just 25 years old, while Joe DiMaggio was 37 at the time.

Their wedding took place at San Francisco City Hall.

The couple’s marriage only lasted nine months, with Marilyn filing for divorce in 1954, according to People.

Arthur Miller, in 1956.

Marilyn’s third and ultimate marriage was with playwright Arthur Miller, and it endured until 1961.

They crossed paths in the early 1950s but only started their relationship after both had divorced their respective partners, as noted by People.

Their wedding took place in a small courthouse in White Plains, New York when Marilyn was 30 and Miller was 40, as reported by Today.

Although Marilyn experienced at least three pregnancies during their marriage, she tragically miscarried each time, and they were unable to have any children together.

Their marriage ultimately ended, and Marilyn passed away shortly after their divorce at the age of 36.
