Who is Geraldo Rivera?

Geraldo Rivera, originally known as Gerald Riviera, is a prominent American journalist, attorney, author, and political commentator.

He had a notable tenure at the Fox News Channel, working from 2001 to 2023.

Rivera is well-known for hosting the tabloid talk show “Geraldo” from 1987 to 1998, which gained significant publicity with the live TV special “The Mystery of Al Capone’s Vaults” in 1986.

Geraldo Rivera Career

During his career, he took on different roles in broadcasting, such as hosting “Geraldo at Large,” and occasionally presenting “Geraldo Rivera Reports” instead of the former.

Between 2022 and 2023, he served as a rotating co-host on the popular show “The Five.”

Throughout his media journey, he made significant contributions to journalism and broadcasting, gaining recognition in American media and political commentary circles.

Why was Geraldo Rivera Fired From Fox News?

Geraldo Rivera, a well-known journalist and political commentator, recently disclosed that he left Fox News because of a difference in “ideology” with the network.

Last month, Fox News let go of Rivera, who was a regular member on The Five.

However, he clarified on Twitter that he ultimately chose to resign from Fox News after his departure from The Five.
